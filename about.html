About Me

Hello, my name is Yunheng Li, though you can call me Leon. I am currently a second-year Manufacturing Engineering student at the University of British Columbia (UBC). My academic focus is on developing advanced technical skills and a deep understanding of modern manufacturing processes, with the goal of contributing to innovative solutions in the industry.

I was born in Shenzhen, China, and moved to Montreal, Canada at the age of 13. This transition exposed me to diverse perspectives and fostered my passion for problem-solving and innovation, which led me to pursue a career in engineering. My experiences in both countries have shaped my approach to challenges, combining a strong technical foundation with a global mindset, and I am eager to contribute to the advancement of the manufacturing industry.

Outside of my academic pursuits, I enjoy a variety of hobbies, including fishing, basketball, video games, and following motorsports like WRC and Formula 1. These interests help me stay balanced and fuel my passion for problem-solving and precision in both my personal and professional life.

Entering the manufacturing program has reaffirmed my passion for the field, particularly through gaining practical experience with advanced machinery such as CNC and water jet cutters. The hands-on training has enhanced my understanding of manufacturing processes and strengthened my commitment to the industry. I am excited to apply these skills in a professional setting and look forward to contributing to innovative projects as I begin my career in the manufacturing industry.
